01 · Growth problem

A lapse should not become abandonment.

The growth opportunity is not to manufacture more clicks. It is to help a motivated beginner convert an initial burst of intent into a repeatable learning routine, while protecting learner trust and educational value.

New learners can complete a first lesson successfully yet still fail to build a stable cue, routine, and reward loop. When motivation fades or a day is missed, the psychological cost of “starting over” can outweigh the perceived value of returning.
Verified fact

Retention is a modeled DAU driver

Duolingo has publicly described Current User Retention Rate as a metric that materially influenced its growth model and A/B testing focus.

User signal

Streaks motivate and pressure

Public feedback shows that streaks can create commitment, but some learners also experience guilt, anxiety, or unhealthy pressure after a lapse.

Planning assumption

The first week is fragile

This case assumes D7 retention is 25% for the selected cohort. The real baseline must be queried before launch and used to recalculate power.

Target cohort

Included: New mobile learners, first course, first completed lesson, no prior streak above two days, eligible market and locale.

Excluded: Existing resurfaced learners, classroom-managed accounts, minors where consent rules require separate treatment, and users in concurrent retention tests.

Growth question

Can a lightweight implementation-intention flow after lesson one increase D7 quality learning retention by at least 1.5 percentage points?

We will not optimize for notification opens, XP accumulation, or raw time in app unless those outcomes also produce meaningful learning activity.

03 · Experiment portfolio

Three hypotheses. One clear first bet.

Each test changes a different behavioral lever: planning, effort reduction, or social accountability. The experiments are independently testable.

Recommended first

Momentum Plan

We believe asking new learners to choose a realistic weekly cadence and preferred study time will increase D7 quality retention because a self-selected plan converts vague intent into a concrete cue.
ControlCurrent post-lesson completion experience
VariantTwo-step cadence and time selection
PrimaryD7 quality learning retention
MDE+1.5 percentage points

Why this is first

It reaches nearly the entire eligible cohort, requires limited backend change, and tests the core behavioral mechanism before adding notification complexity or network effects.

What could falsify it

If completion of the post-lesson flow drops, reminders are ignored, or D7 lift disappears after controlling for plan completion, the intervention may be adding ceremony rather than commitment.

What stays out of the first test

A redesigned XP economy, a streak-loss countdown, or additional guilt-based notifications. Each introduces broader behavioral and brand effects before validating the basic commitment mechanism.

Core tradeoff

A larger intervention may create a larger lift, but slower learning. The first test deliberately favors reach, reversibility, clean attribution, and low operational complexity.

05 · Metric system

Measure learning retention, not attention capture.

The metric tree prevents a superficially positive result from shipping when it harms learning quality, user trust, or downstream behavior.

Primary outcome

D7 Quality Learning Retention

Percentage of eligible learners who complete at least one qualifying learning session on day seven after activation.

D7_QLR = users_with_qualifying_session_on_D7 ÷ eligible_activated_users
⇄
Mechanism metricsPlan completion, reminder scheduling, return at selected time
Secondary outcomesD2/D3 retention, 7-day quality sessions, voluntary sessions
Learning guardrailTime Spent Learning Well per retained learner
Trust guardrailsNotification opt-outs, app uninstalls, complaint rate
Business guardrailTrial or subscription conversion, reported separately

Qualifying session

Contains meaningful learning interactions, passes anti-gaming checks, and meets the internal learning-quality definition. Merely opening the app does not count.

Novelty effect

Read D7 first, then inspect D14 and D28 directional trends. A short-term lift that disappears immediately should not be presented as durable habit formation.

Segment reads

Pre-register locale, age-policy group, notification permission, course type, device, acquisition source, and initial proficiency. Avoid post-hoc winner hunting.

06 · Experiment architecture

Assignment, delivery, telemetry, decision.

The technical design keeps treatment deterministic, instrumentation auditable, and rollout reversible.

1. Eligibility serviceNew learner rules, exclusions, consent, concurrent-test checks
2. Assignment serviceStable user hash, 50/50 split, experiment namespace
3. Remote configurationVariant payload, copy, timing rules, kill switch
4. Event pipelineClient events → stream → warehouse → quality labels
5. Statistics layerSRM checks, effect estimate, confidence interval, guardrails

Assignment logic

if eligible(user, "momentum_plan_v1"): bucket = stable_hash(user.id, experiment.id) % 100 variant = "control" if bucket < 50 else "momentum_plan" log_assignment(user.id, experiment.id, variant) return remote_config(variant) else: return current_experience()

Data quality controls

• Sample-ratio mismatch alert before outcome analysis

• Assignment event emitted server-side and deduplicated

• Exposure recorded only when the learner can actually see the treatment

• Event versioning and schema contracts for every client release

• Holdout integrity checks across concurrent lifecycle experiments

09 · Decision framework

Pre-commit the ship, hold, and kill rules.

Statistical significance alone is insufficient. The product decision combines effect size, uncertainty, mechanism evidence, and guardrail health.

Illustrative decision
SHIP GRADUALLY

Primary outcome clears the minimum bar, statistical confidence is acceptable, and guardrails remain healthy. Ramp 10% → 50% → 100% with live monitoring.

This simulator is a portfolio artifact, not a substitute for Duolingo’s internal statistical standards, multiple-testing policy, or legal review.

Ship

Lift ≥ 1.5 pp, p < .05, no material TSLW decline, notification opt-out increase < 0.5 pp, and no severe segment harm.

Investigate

Positive but underpowered result, heterogeneous segment effects, weak mechanism adoption, or a small guardrail warning that may be mitigated.

Kill

Negative primary outcome, material trust or learning-quality harm, sample-ratio mismatch unresolved, or treatment effect dependent on manipulative pressure.

11 · Delivery plan

From evidence to reversible rollout.

A six-week plan aligns product, design, engineering, data science, lifecycle, privacy, and support around one decision.

Week 1

Diagnose and pre-register

Validate baseline funnel, define quality session, document exclusions, lock hypotheses, metrics, segments, and decision thresholds.

Week 2

Prototype and usability test

Test comprehension, perceived pressure, cadence choices, accessibility, and skip behavior with a small research panel.

Week 3

Build and instrument

Implement remote configuration, assignment, event contracts, QA dashboards, feature flag, and automated guardrail alerts.

Week 4

Internal and 1% rollout

Dogfood, verify event parity, run SRM checks, test rollback, inspect crash and latency metrics, and review support readiness.

Week 5

50/50 experiment

Enroll for two weekly cycles, freeze nonessential changes, monitor only pre-specified safety signals, and avoid outcome peeking.

Week 6+

Analyze and decide

Read intention-to-treat results, confidence intervals, mechanism metrics, guardrails, segments, novelty effects, and rollout recommendation.

12 · Scope and tradeoffs

Senior PM judgment is visible in what stays out.

The experiment is deliberately narrow enough to learn quickly and broad enough to matter.

In scope

• New mobile learner cohort

• Self-selected weekly cadence and time

• Existing reminder infrastructure where possible

• D7 retention and learning-quality instrumentation

• Remote-configured copy and kill switch

Out of scope

• Rebuilding streak mechanics or XP economy

• Pricing, paywall, or subscription packaging changes

• Personalized lesson-ranking model changes

• Classroom or school-managed users

• Global launch before locale and policy validation

Speed vs depth

A two-step plan is less personalized than a fully adaptive habit coach, but it is faster to test and easier to attribute.

Reach vs precision

Broad eligibility improves power, while pre-specified segment analysis protects against hiding meaningful harm or non-response.

Retention vs autonomy

The design supports follow-through without treating every missed day as failure or escalating emotional pressure.

13 · Interview story

The 90-second executive readout.

Lead with the growth problem and decision logic, not the interface.

“The work focuses on the first-week retention problem, not on adding another gamification feature. Duolingo has publicly shown that current-user retention is a meaningful growth lever, while its streak and social mechanics demonstrate the power of routine and accountability. The proposal tests whether a learner-selected cadence and study time can convert initial motivation into a repeatable cue. The primary metric is Day-7 quality learning retention, with Time Spent Learning Well and notification opt-outs as guardrails. This path is prioritized over a rescue notification and a buddy sprint because it has higher reach, lower effort, cleaner attribution, and fewer network effects. The feature should ship only if the test clears a 1.5-point lift, remains statistically credible, and produces no material learning-quality or trust harm.”
