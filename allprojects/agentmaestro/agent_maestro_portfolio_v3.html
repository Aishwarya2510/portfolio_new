Case-study thesis

AI becomes valuable when it is embedded into the operating model.

The concept is deliberately broader than a chatbot. It redesigns how decisions are requested, evaluated, approved, executed, and learned from while preserving human judgment for material or high-risk choices.

01

Structure ambiguity

Convert loosely framed requests into a consistent decision schema with objective, urgency, evidence needs, affected functions, and desired outcome.

02

Ground judgment

Retrieve the current policy, relevant data, prior decisions, contractual obligations, and operational dependencies before options are assessed.

03

Govern risk

Apply thresholds, approvals, exception handling, confidence requirements, and human review based on decision materiality.

04

Close the loop

Translate the decision into owned actions, track outcomes, preserve the rationale, and learn which assumptions or controls were effective.

Product operating principles

Business logic first. Language enhancement second.

The system is deliberately bounded. Core decisions remain explainable, permissioned, and auditable; generative AI improves interpretation and communication only after the decision logic is established.

01 - Bound

Clear domain scope

Reject requests outside approved operational workflows before retrieval or model use.

02 - Ground

Policy-backed evidence

Use current SOPs, matrices, process maps, known issues, and templates as the evidence packet.

03 - Control

Deterministic governance

Keep risk tiers, approval thresholds, ownership rules, and exceptions reviewable.

04 - Enhance

Optional LLM layer

Use AI to classify ambiguity and improve narrative quality without surrendering decision rights.

05 - Learn

Operational memory

Capture confidence, feedback, overrides, failures, and backlog signals after every decision run.

Project justification

This project exists because operational decisions are rarely owned by one system.

Agent Maestro is justified by a practical enterprise gap: teams have tools, documents, experts, and workflows, but no shared decision layer that connects evidence, risk, ownership, approval, and execution into one auditable run.

Why it matters

AI alone does not solve cross-functional operations.

A chatbot can summarize a ticket, but it cannot prove which policy applies, who owns the decision, what approval threshold was triggered, which evidence is missing, or how execution should be tracked. Agent Maestro is designed around that operating gap.

Evidence is scattered: policies, SOPs, approval matrices, account history, invoices, Slack context, and prior decisions live in separate places.
Risk is inconsistent: materiality, customer impact, regulatory exposure, and policy conflict are judged differently by different teams.
Ownership is unclear: requests stall when Finance, Legal, Support, Product, and Operations each own only part of the answer.
Learning is lost: after a decision is made, the rationale, overrides, and outcome rarely become reusable operating memory.
What the project proves

A governed decision layer can be designed, measured, and built.

The project demonstrates how to move from an ambiguous business request to a controlled decision package using intake structure, retrieval, scoring, approval routing, execution ownership, and feedback loops.

Business design: maps current-state friction into a future-state operating model.
System design: separates source systems, orchestration logic, AI interpretation, governance controls, and audit storage.
Risk design: keeps risk, confidence, and option value as separate concepts instead of one opaque score.
Implementation design: defines APIs, roles, logs, evaluation signals, connectors, and a rollout plan.

Operating-model overlay

Agent Maestro sits above existing systems and governs the decision between them.

It does not replace Salesforce, Zendesk, ERP, policy repositories, or functional teams. It creates a common decision layer that interprets the request, assembles evidence, applies controls, and routes accountable execution back into those systems.

Preserve

Systems of record remain authoritative

Customer, invoice, policy, approval, and task data stay in their governed source systems.

Coordinate

The decision layer resolves cross-system ambiguity

Agent Maestro creates one evidence packet and one trace across workflows that normally fragment between functions.

Control

Execution returns through permissioned workflows

The platform recommends and routes. Approved systems and authorized humans complete material business actions.

Multi-agent operating architecture

Five specialized agents, one governed decision core.

Each agent has a bounded responsibility. Deterministic rules control scope, risk, ownership, and approval; AI is used where interpretation or synthesis adds value.

K

Knowledge Agent

Retrieves policy, SOP, known-issue, process-map, and template context.

Produces

Relevant snippets, source list, versions, contradictions, and missing-context flags.

A

Tests the request and evidence for control gaps, failure patterns, and root causes.

Produces

Audit findings, evidence gaps, control issues, and improvement opportunities.

W

Workflow Agent

Converts findings into an executable sequence with owners and dependencies.

Produces

Next steps, accountable owner, timeline, escalation path, and handoffs.

R

Finance Risk Agent

Evaluates amount, customer impact, policy conflict, and approval thresholds.

Produces

Risk tier, score, approval owner, manual-review requirement, and governance note.

E

Executive Summary Agent

Turns the governed outputs into leadership-ready communication.

Produces

Recommendation, rationale, decision brief, stakeholder update, and source trace.

Scoring design

Risk, confidence, and option value answer different questions.

The system avoids one blended "AI score." Risk determines governance, confidence determines how much evidence or review is required, and option scoring supports the business tradeoff after minimum controls are satisfied.

Framework 1 - Governance routing

Risk score

Converts the material signals in a request into a consistent review tier. The weights are configurable by workflow and policy owner.

Risk = Sum (signal severity x approved weight)
Financial exposure25%
Customer / regulatory sensitivity20%
Policy conflict15%
Evidence gap15%
Multi-team dependency15%
Urgency / irreversibility10%
0-29 - Routine 30-54 - Controlled 55-79 - High review 80-100 - Critical
Framework 2 - Automation boundary

Confidence-to-action mapping

Confidence is kept separate from risk. A confident answer can still be too material to automate, while a low-risk request can still require more evidence.

Lower risk
Higher risk
High confidence
RouteRecommend, log, lightweight approval
EscalateRecommend with mandatory human approval
Low confidence
ClarifyRequest missing evidence or expert input
BlockManual queue; no automated action

Critical rule

Confidence never cancels material risk. Any critical-risk case remains human-controlled regardless of model certainty.

Framework 3 - Business tradeoffs

Option value score

Compares feasible options only after policy, evidence, and minimum-control gates are satisfied.

Option score = Sum (criterion weight x option performance) / total weights
Financial value and cost-to-serve
Customer impact and service continuity
Operational feasibility and dependency load
Risk-adjusted exposure
Speed to value and reversibility
Framework 4 - Governed learning

Outcome and override signals

The platform learns through controlled review rather than silently changing business rules after each decision.

Compare recommended versus approved path
Capture override reason and decision authority
Measure outcome against original assumptions
Create a policy, taxonomy, or workflow backlog item
Require owner approval before logic changes
